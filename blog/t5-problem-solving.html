Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you got blocked on a simple problem

During my first week and first sprint in phase zero we were tasked with moving a file (named after a fruit) to another location (in a peer's folder) using git hub and the command line. I tried a handful of times before admitting that I needed help. I had seen that one of my peers had already completed the task as they had completed the task and moved their fruit file into my folder. I reached out on slack and sent her a message to ask her for some help. Throughout the problem I was getting frustrated as I knew it was a simple task and I shouldn’t have been struggling with it. I knew it would be something very small that I was missing.

I learnt that there is no harm asking your peers for help. I probably wasted more time than was necessary trying to figure it out myself when asking my peer for help took no time at all and she was more than happy to help me. Turns out it was something very minor that I was missing.

Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you solved a problem in an elegant way

After the first two sprints I had made some good progress on my html and css pages, however the code was starting to look quite messy and my css had quite a few double ups. I sat down for a few hours and worked on my psuedocoding to try and break each step down and write comments as to what I was trying to do. This helped tidy up my code. Throughout the process it felt very satisfying to tidy everything and add some order to my code. I learnt that you should always psuedocode to make things more readable and easier to edit when necessary.
