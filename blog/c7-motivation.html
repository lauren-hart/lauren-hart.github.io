How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

Seeking out pleasure is something we priortise for happiness. When I think about things that I do now for pleasure such as buying a new handbag. I get pleasure for a very short amount of time and then that pleasure wears off and I want to go and buy something else. It is easy to see why this sort of happiness is unstustainable.

I have quite often found myself in a state of "flow" (or passion) where I am at work and I am doing a task I found immensly satisfying. Time goes by very quickly and I come away with a deep feeling of satisfaction. But that is hard to come by and there is always going to be tasks that I don't get the same feeling of flow with.

When you think about life through the purpose lens, it is easy to see how you can sustain a level of happiness. It is a much more broad way of thinking and by referring back to what you value most during every task as long as what you are doing will somehow contribute to your greater purpose you can be happy in knowing that the passion and pleasure will come as a result.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise -

What are your core values?

Acting with integrity, being trustworthy and being kind.

What do you stand for?

Being acknowledged for doing a good job, helping others out when they need it and always being honest with yourself.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

It isn't nice thinking about times when you have failed, but it made sense to just observe them as simply experiences. That made it easier to not feel strong emotion towards the failures. When envisioning your successes you do notice a slight rise in your mood and it felt more difficult to let go of the happy emotion I was feeling.

Any other musings?

It was quite enjoyable to dream up a way of how you would like to live. I think it helps to pinpoint the things that you would like to change now but haven't been motivated to do so. By writing down what you would like to improve in your life you can recognise what needs to be changed and start making changes now to get to where you want to be.
